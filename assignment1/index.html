Interactive Media Assignment 1

The website I chose is:
https://brooomstick.com/

1) What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

When I was scrolling through the site, I noticed the graphics of the site move. The graphics are tied to where the user is in terms of scroll distance, and it evolves as the user progresses through the site.

2) Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

Most of the time during the two minutes, I scrolled, watched the graphics form, and read the text. I do not think I was doing much else other than processing information, which I think was the whole point of a site such as this.

3) What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

Learning about what janitors do and what they had to go through, as well as learning what other forms of how janitors are venerated, was the part I spent the most time engaging with. That is to be expected, since that seems to be the only experience on the site. Playing around with the scrollbar was secondary to that experience.

4) What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

Scrolling to progress through the graphics was the most common action, followed by reading the text about janitors. Also expected, since the website is revolved around those two actions to give information to the user.

5) What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

I think the interactive's primary goal was well achieved. It gave clear and concise information about janitors, and how they are venerated. I did not even know about how people back then painted artworks of janitors.

6) How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

It communicates this experience via tying graphics to the scrollbar and using big texts. As the user scrolls, the webpage itself evolves to give way to the next sections of the site. Since this is primarily a site dedicated to raise awareness of janitors of the modern world, big and simple texts are used to effectively deliver information to the user.

7) What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

The experience should be interacted for about two to three minutes. The user mainly scrolls and reads through the site to receive information. Acknowledging that and because of the nature of the site, I think the experience should be interacted with once. Once the user gets to the end, there really is nothing else extra to experience, other than going back to review those same experiences again.

8) How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

The lack of personalisation and mechanisms for the user to engage with it regularly is how the site communicates that intended length of interaction. The scrolling itself not offering to have the user to come back also contributes to this communication.

9) What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

I believe that this media reminds me of a phamphlet of sorts - the kind that gives you some sort of information that is often overlooked. In this case, janitor's jobs and importance to society is something that everyone, in the modern era, overlooks. The structure of the sections also resemble them in away: first, it introduces you to the overlooked information, then goes in depth to how important it is to be aware of. It also reminds me of those advertisements that raise awareness of an overlooked aspect of society.

10) What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

For this kind of site, user engagement is a must, so graphics tend to be valued quite greatly. Users tend to stay when there is something going on -- most users do not really stay on informational sites that are mostly walls of text unless they are already very interested in the content itself. These sites that raise awareness has to catch the user's attention to give the users information. How phamphlets and advertisements engage the user is through visual elements accompanying the information, so the site should relatively do the same.

11) What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

Informational sites, phamphlets and advertisements usually cater to its intended audience with a visual style that typically engages them. Some advertisements (such as bad effects of cigarettes) appeal to the user by telling a story to evoke sadness and pity, which in turn affects them to not consume cigarettes. In general, persuasive and informative media generally appeal to the user's humanity and empathy to drive their point home.

12) What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

The most frustrating part that I could think of for this site is the fact that every graphic is tied to the scroll bar. I can see it being tedious to get to some sections or see specific graphics that I wanted to see. Due to the amount of graphics in the site moving, it could also be quite overwhelming if the user is trying to go back and forth to re-read.

13) What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

The most satisfying part, as well, is the graphics, as it is the focus of the site alongside the information given. The time in between the texts allows the user to digest the information given, while keeping them engaged through the scrollbar graphic animations. It is satisfying to watch the graphics fly by to create a cohesive image.
